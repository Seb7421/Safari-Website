Evenings along Lake Malawi are gentle, luminous, and unhurried. After a day exploring villages or snorkelling through shoals of jewel-toned cichlids, we gather our travellers for simple rituals that honour the lake and its people.

## Sandbank sundowners

We boat to a nearby sandbank with a portable fire pit, fresh mango juice, and roasted groundnuts sourced that morning. Local storytellers join us to share lake legends while the water glows copper around the dhow silhouettes.

## Stargazing on the deck

Far from city lights, the Milky Way drapes itself over the lake. We provide star charts annotated by our astronomy-loving guides so you can trace the Southern Cross before turning in to the hush of the shoreline.

## Giving back together

Before the night ends we assemble school supply packs for the next day's community visit. It's a quiet act that connects each journey with long-term partners working to protect the lake's fragile ecosystem.
